Water and the Water Cycle Page

Water is a vital resource that exists in three states: solid (ice), liquid (water), and gas (vapor).
The water cycle, or hydrological cycle,
describes the continuous movement of water on, above, and below Earth’s surface.
It begins with evaporation, where water from oceans, rivers,
and lakes turns into vapor due to the sun’s heat.
Transpiration from plants also adds water vapor to the atmosphere.
As the vapor rises and cools, condensation occurs, forming clouds that eventually lead to precipitation
in the form of rain, snow, or hail. Some of this water infiltrates the ground,
recharging aquifers, while the rest flows as runoff into rivers and oceans.
The cycle is crucial for distributing fresh water, regulating climate, and
sustaining ecosystems worldwide.
