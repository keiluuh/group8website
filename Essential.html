Essential Elements

Carbon


A naturally abundant, nonmetallic element that occurs in all organic compounds and can be found in all known forms of life.

Hydrogen


a nonmetallic element that is the simplest and lightest of the elements and that is normally a colorless odorless highly flammable diatomic gas.

Oxygen

Nitrogen


a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas that is the most plentiful element in Earth's atmosphere and is a constituent of all living matter.

Phosphorus


Used to make fertilisers, animal feeds, rust removers, corrosion preventers, and even dishwasher tablets.

Sulfur


the chemical element of atomic number 15, a poisonous, combustible nonmetal which exists in two common allotropic forms, white phosphorus, a yellowish waxy solid which ignites spontaneously in air and glows in the dark, and red phosphorus, a less reactive form used in making matches.
